**Title:** I spent 6 months researching timeshare exit options — here's what I learned

**Body:**

If you're trying to get out of a timeshare, the hardest part is figuring out what's real and what's a scam. The exit industry is a minefield.

**What actually works (in my experience):**

1. **Professional exit representation** — A legitimate company that handles the entire process: evaluates your contract, negotiates with the resort, handles paperwork. This was the path that worked for me and most owners I talked to. The key is finding one that's actually legitimate (see red flags below).

2. **Legal cancellation** — If the resort misrepresented something at the time of sale, a timeshare attorney can sometimes force cancellation. This only works in specific circumstances, but a good professional can tell you in the first call if you have a case.

3. **Transfer/give away** — If you have a desirable resort and week, you MIGHT find someone to take it. But most timeshares sit unsold for months or years even at $1.

**How to spot a scam (before they take your money):**
- Upfront fees over $1,500 before any work is done
- "100% guarantee" — no one can guarantee this
- Pressure to sign today ("this offer expires")
- No physical address or refuses to put terms in writing
- Can't name a single attorney they work with
