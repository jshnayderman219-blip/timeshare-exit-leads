How to Exit Your Timeshare Legally

A no-nonsense guide for owners who want out — without getting scammed.

⚠️ First: Beware of Exit Scams
The FTC warns that many "timeshare exit companies" are scams. They charge $3,000–$10,000 upfront, promise to cancel your contract, then disappear. Never pay large upfront fees to anyone. Legitimate exits cost far less — or nothing — depending on your situation.

Your Exit Options (Best → Worst)

1. Resort Deed-Back / Surrender Program

Cost: $0–$1,500 | Many major resort chains now offer formal exit programs:

  • Wyndham — Certified Exit program (free for paid-off contracts)
  • Marriott Vacation Club — Resale Deed-Back program
  • Hilton Grand Vacations — Transitions program
  • Holiday Inn Club — Horizons exit program
  • Diamond Resorts — Transitions program
  • Westgate — Legacy program for eligible owners
  • Bluegreen — Deed-back for paid-off contracts

Call your resort directly and ask: "Do you have a deed-back or surrender program?" If your loan is paid off and maintenance fees are current, this is your fastest path out — often at zero cost.

2. Transfer to a New Owner (Give It Away)

Cost: $100–$500 (transfer/closing fees) | If your resort doesn't offer deed-back, giving away your timeshare on the secondary market may work:

  • List it for $1 on eBay (timeshares for sale category)
  • Post in the TUGBBS Free Timeshares forum (tugbbs.com)
  • List on RedWeek.com and SellMyTimeshareNow.com
  • Offer to pay the new owner's first year of maintenance fees as an incentive
  • Use a licensed timeshare closing company to handle the transfer (LT Transfers, Timeshare Closing Services)
Warning: Even "free" timeshares are hard to give away. Do not pay anyone an upfront fee to "market" or "list" your timeshare — you can do this yourself for free.

3. Sell on the Resale Market

Likely recovery: 0–10% of original purchase price. Most timeshares resell for pennies on the dollar. A unit you paid $25,000 for may sell for $1 on eBay. Be realistic. If someone offers you $50,000 for your timeshare — it's a scam.

4. Stop Paying (Last Resort — Understand the Risks)

Stopping maintenance fee payments will trigger foreclosure. Consequences:

  • Credit score impact — the resort reports to credit bureaus
  • Collections — unpaid fees go to debt collectors
  • Foreclosure — the resort takes back the timeshare through legal action
  • No deficiency judgment in some states (Florida, California) — but this varies

Speak with a consumer protection attorney before stopping payments. A consultation costs $200–$400 — far less than years of maintenance fees.

✅ Before You Do Anything — Document Your Timeshare
  1. Find your original purchase contract
  2. Confirm: is the mortgage/loan paid off?
  3. Note: how much are annual maintenance fees?
  4. Check: what state is the timeshare in? (laws vary by state)
  5. Call the resort: ask about deed-back or surrender programs

What a Legitimate Exit Professional Actually Does

If you can't exit through the resort directly, a legitimate exit professional may help by:

  • Negotiating directly with the resort on your behalf
  • Identifying contract violations that allow legal cancellation
  • Working with a timeshare attorney (not just a "relief company")
  • Handling the deed transfer paperwork correctly
  • Providing a clear timeline and guarantee in writing

Red flags to avoid: upfront fees over $1,500, guarantee of "100% exit," pressure to sign immediately, refusal to put terms in writing, no physical office address.
